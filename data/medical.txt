What is diabetes?
Diabetes is a chronic condition where the body cannot properly regulate blood sugar levels.

What are the symptoms of diabetes?
Common symptoms include frequent urination, excessive thirst, fatigue, and blurred vision.

What is fever?
Fever is a temporary increase in body temperature, often due to an infection.

What are common causes of fever?
Fever is commonly caused by infections such as flu, viral illnesses, or bacterial infections.

What is hypertension?
Hypertension is a medical condition in which blood pressure in the arteries is persistently elevated.

What are the symptoms of high blood pressure?
High blood pressure often has no symptoms, but severe cases may cause headaches or dizziness.

What is anemia?
Anemia is a condition in which the blood lacks enough healthy red blood cells to carry adequate oxygen.

What are the symptoms of anemia?
Symptoms include weakness, fatigue, pale skin, and shortness of breath.
